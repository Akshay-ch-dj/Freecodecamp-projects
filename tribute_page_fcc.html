Michael Faraday

Father of Electromagnetism

Time line of Michael Faraday's life:

  • 1791 - Born in Newington Butts, London, he is the 3rd child in the family, got only the most basic school education in childhood.

  • 1805 - At the age of 14, he become an apprentice to George Riebau, a local bookbinder and bookseller in Blandford Street, London. In his 7-year apprenticeship here, Faraday got a chance to read many books, educated himself, also developed an interest in science.

  • 1812 - At the age of 20 Faraday attended lectures by the eminent English chemist Humphry Davy of Royal Institution, England, which set a turning point in his life. Faraday sent Davy his notes taken during the lectures, accompanied with a letter asking for employment in the field, Davy got impressed by his scientific temper.

  • 1813 - Davy employed Faraday as his assistant, later he elevated as a chemical assistant at the Royal Institution. It is often jokingly said of Davy that Faraday was, in hindsight, his greatest ever discovery.

  • 1816 - Working with Davy in Chemistry, Faraday delivered chemistry lectures for City philosophical society, which refined the quality of his lectures.

  • 1820 - Faraday himself had become one of the world's most premier chemists of the time. His labors under Davy had given him a vast amount of experience, Faraday set out on his own works. He succeeded in creating the first known compounds of chlorine and carbon C2Cl6 and C2Cl4.

  • 1821 - Faraday married Sarah Barnard, same year for further experimenting Davy's research about electricity and magnetism, Faraday build two devices to produce what he called "electromagnetic rotation"- laid the foundation of modern electromagnetic technology, but due to a misunderstanding between Davy and Faraday he did not able to continue his work.

  • 1823 - Michael Faraday succeeded liquefaction of ammonia(built on the ideas of John Dalton), notices the cooling effect when ammonia evaporates. Faraday's work had shown that mechanical pumps could be used to turn gas to liquid at room temperature, this laid a milestone in the development of modern refrigeration techniques.

  • 1824 - He made experiments that perfected the Bunsen burner. Also as a fact, The first rubber balloons were made by him for use in his experiments with hydrogen at the Royal Institution.

  • 1825 - Michael was working on illuminating gases and succeeded in isolating and describing something that would later be known as benzene. Around this time he also helped lay down the foundations of metallurgy and metallography whilst conducting investigations into steel alloys.

  • 1826 - He worked on an assignment by the Royal Society into improving the quality of glasses and telescopes, succeeded in producing a very high refractive index that would later help him discover diamagnetism.

  • 1831 - After the death of Davy, he continued his series of experiments that led to the discovery of Electromagnetic Induction, he also experimented in Mutual Induction( which later modelled mathematically by Maxwell), using his iron-ring-coil apparatus, it can be considered as an initial model of Transformer, later he discovered Electric Dynamo, ancestor of modern power generator and electric motor.

  • 1832 - In the apex period of his scientific excellence, he turned down all the offered honors said he preferred to live "plain Mr. Faraday till the End".

  • 1833 - Based on the electrochemical research he published Faraday's laws of electrolysis, also invented the system of oxidation numbers, and popularized terminology such as anode, cathode, electrode, and ion.

  • 1836 - Invented Faraday Cage - enclosure used to block electromagnetic fields, the idea found extensive use even in today's sensible electronic equipment protection, microwaves etc.

  • 1839 - Concept of lines of Flux, which is a crucial idea helped in the coming age developments in Electromagnetism.

  • 1845 - Discovered Diamagnetism and magneto-optical effect, today known as the Faraday effect.(rotation of plane polarized light with magnetic field).

  • 1848 - The famous lecture of "chemical history of a candle" - one of his 19 Christmas lectures, started in 1925 to present science to general people. The joyful lectures showcasing experiments and deep philosophies helped developing scientific thinking in people, and still given in the Royal Institution every year.

  • 1858 - Retired from Royal Institution to live in the Master Mason's house which later called Faraday house.

  • 1867 - Died in 1867 at the age of 75. He had previously turned down burial in Westminster Abbey, but he has a memorial plaque there, near Isaac Newton's tomb. The unit of capacitance, Farad, given in honor of Michael Faraday.

Michael Faraday was a highly influential British scientist who in part turned electricity into something that could be harnessed for work, his works ultimately lead to our modern-day understanding of electromagnetism. He is one of history's most accomplished experimental scientists, Albert Einstein actually kept photos of three scientists in his office.. Isaac Newton, James Clerk Maxwell and, Michael Faraday.
